# Shopify Catalog Health Checker: Pre-MVP Validation Report

Prepared for: Product validation sprint
Date: June 25, 2026
Goal: Validate demand before building the full Shopify app

## 1. Executive Summary

We want to validate a Shopify app idea before committing weeks of engineering work.

The product idea is a Catalog Health Checker for Shopify stores. It scans a merchant's product catalog and finds issues that can affect SEO, conversion, shopping feed quality, customer trust, and store operations.

The full app may eventually check and fix:

- Missing or weak product descriptions
- Missing SEO titles and meta descriptions
- Missing image alt text
- Weak product titles
- Duplicate product titles
- Missing SKUs
- Duplicate SKUs
- Missing product weights
- Missing vendors or product types
- Poor tagging and collection organization
- Variant naming issues
- Google Shopping feed readiness issues

For the pre-MVP stage, we will not build the full Shopify app yet. Instead, we will validate the idea with a landing page, mock screenshots, manual 10-product audits, outreach to Shopify store owners, and direct payment-interest questions.

The main question to answer is:

Would Shopify merchants pay $19-$49/month for an app that scans their catalog, shows product data issues, and fixes many of them in bulk after approval?

## 2. Why This Pre-MVP Exists

Building the full Shopify app too early is risky. We need evidence that store owners care enough to respond, request an audit, and consider paying.

The pre-MVP helps us test:

- Whether merchants understand the problem
- Whether the landing page message is clear
- Whether merchants accept a free audit
- Which catalog issues they care about most
- Whether $19-$49/month feels acceptable
- Which niche responds best
- What objections appear repeatedly
- Whether the product should focus on SEO, catalog cleanup, returns, Google Shopping readiness, or all of them

This phase should be cheap, fast, and practical.

## 3. Target Product Positioning

Primary positioning:

Catalog Health Checker for Shopify Stores

Plain-English promise:

Find and fix missing SEO fields, weak product descriptions, missing image alt text, duplicate SKUs, and messy product data before they cost you sales.

Alternative positioning lines:

- Clean up your Shopify catalog in minutes, not days.
- Find weak product pages and fix them in bulk.
- Improve product data quality before it hurts search, ads, shipping, or conversion.
- A product data quality assistant for Shopify merchants.

Avoid positioning:

- AI product description generator
- ChatGPT for Shopify
- AI writer for ecommerce

Reason:

Those sound generic and easy to replace with ChatGPT. The stronger value is catalog scanning, issue detection, bulk workflow, and safe Shopify integration.

## 4. Ideal First Customer Segment

Start with one niche. Do not target all Shopify stores immediately.

Recommended first niche:

Fashion and apparel Shopify stores.

Why:

- Many products and variants
- Size, color, material, and fit data matter
- Product descriptions are often weak or duplicated
- Image alt text and SEO are common gaps
- Returns can be high when product information is unclear
- Merchants understand product presentation

Backup niches:

- Beauty and skincare
- Jewelry
- Home decor and furniture
- Supplements
- Electronics accessories

Do not start with:

- Very large enterprise stores
- Stores on Shopify Plus only
- Stores with very few products
- Dropshipping stores with low-quality copied catalogs, unless we intentionally target them later

## 5. Pre-MVP Offer

The offer should be simple:

Free 10-product catalog audit for your Shopify store.

What the audit includes:

- A quick health score
- Issues found across 10 selected products
- Before and after examples
- Suggested improvements
- Recommendation on whether bulk cleanup would help

The merchant does not need to install anything. We only need their public store URL.

Optional CTA:

Join the early beta list for the bulk catalog cleanup tool.

## 6. Validation Goals

Run the first validation sprint against 100 Shopify stores.

Success targets:

- 100 personalized outreaches sent
- 20 or more replies
- 10 or more audit requests
- 5 or more strong-interest responses
- 2 or more people willing to join beta
- At least 3 people say they would consider paying $19-$49/month

Strong interest means:

- They ask when it will be ready
- They ask for pricing
- They share more store issues
- They ask if it can fix all products
- They offer to test it
- They say the audit is useful

Failure signals:

- Very few replies after 100 personalized messages
- People say the problem is not important
- People only want free work
- People would not pay more than a few dollars
- The niche does not understand the pain

If validation is weak, change one variable at a time:

- Niche
- Offer
- Landing page headline
- Outreach script
- Audit format
- Problem focus

## 11. Manual Audit Process

Inputs needed from merchant:

- Store URL
- Optional niche/category
- Optional product collection they care about

Inputs from us:

- Select 10 public product pages
- Review product content manually
- Use AI only as assistant, not as automated system

Usually visible from public storefront:

- Product title
- Product description
- Product images
- Product price
- Variant labels
- Page title
- Meta description, if available in page source
- URL slug
- Collection/category context

Not always visible:

- SKU
- Product weight
- Shopify internal tags
- Vendor
- Product type
- Admin SEO fields
- Image alt text may require page inspection

Because we cannot see everything publicly, phrase the audit carefully:

Public storefront audit, not full Shopify admin scan.

Audit checklist:

- Product title clarity
- Product description completeness
- SEO meta description
- Product image context
- URL slug quality
- Variant clarity
- Trust and conversion clarity
- Overall issue severity

Audit report format:

- Store name and URL
- Date
- Audit summary
- Catalog health estimate
- Top 5 issues
- Product-by-product findings
- Before/after examples
- Bulk cleanup opportunity
- Payment interest question
- Next step CTA

## 12. AI Usage During Pre-MVP

At this stage, AI can help generate:

- Improved product titles
- Better product descriptions
- SEO meta descriptions
- Image alt text suggestions from visible context
- Audit summaries
- Before/after examples
- Merchant-specific recommendations

Do not use AI blindly.

Manual review is still needed because:

- We need quality control
- Public data may be incomplete
- We are learning customer pain
- The goal is validation, not automation yet

Suggested AI prompt:

```text
You are helping audit Shopify product pages for catalog quality, SEO, and conversion clarity. Review the provided product title, description, URL, visible image context, and category. Identify issues, rate severity, and suggest improved title, description, and meta description. Keep suggestions commercially realistic and avoid exaggerated claims.
```

## 16. Pricing Validation

Do not ask only:

Would you pay?

Ask better questions:

- What would make this worth $29/month?
- Would this be more valuable as a weekly audit or a one-time cleanup?
- Would you prefer monthly subscription or pay-per-cleanup?
- How many products would you need to fix each month?
- Would you pay more for bulk updates?

Potential pricing:

- Free: limited scan, view issues, few fixes
- Starter: $19/month, 500 products scanned, 100 AI fixes/month
- Growth: $49/month, 2,000 products scanned, 500 AI fixes/month, bulk fix workflow
- Pro: $99/month, larger catalog, weekly scans, export reports, more AI fixes

## 20. Decision Criteria

Proceed to MVP if:

- At least 10 merchants request audits
- At least 5 show strong interest
- At least 2 agree to beta test
- Several merchants confirm $19-$49/month is reasonable
- The same pain points repeat across stores

Do not proceed yet if:

- Most people ignore it
- People say the problem is not important
- People want only free manual work
- People would not install a Shopify app for this
- The value feels unclear

Pivot options:

- Focus only on SEO cleanup
- Focus on Google Shopping feed readiness
- Focus on fashion catalog quality
- Focus on returns reduction through better product data
- Focus on agency dashboards

## 21. Risks And Mitigations

Risk: Merchants think this is just AI writing.

Mitigation:

Position as catalog health, data quality, and bulk cleanup.

Risk: Public audits cannot see full Shopify admin data.

Mitigation:

Call it a public storefront audit and explain the full app would scan deeper after installation.

Risk: Low reply rate.

Mitigation:

Personalize messages and mention one visible issue from their store.

Risk: People like the free audit but will not pay.

Mitigation:

Ask payment questions directly after showing value.

Risk: Too broad.

Mitigation:

Start with one niche, preferably fashion/apparel.

Risk: Competitors exist.

Mitigation:

Differentiate through catalog health score, bulk workflow, niche focus, and quality of recommendations.
